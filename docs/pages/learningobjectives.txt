<h1 id="learning-objectives">Learning Objectives</h1>

<h2>General</h2>
<ul>
<li>Ability to compare qualitative and quantitative methods and name situations where each would be appropriate</li>
<li>Awareness of the standard process of image processing, the steps involved, and the typical order in which they take place</li>
<li>Ability to create and evaluate quantitative metrics to compare the success of different approaches/processes/workflows</li>
<li>Appreciation of automation and which steps it is most appropriate for</li>
<li>The relationship between automation and reproducibility for analysis</li>
</ul>


<h2>Image Enhancement</h2>
<ul>
<li>Awareness of the function enhancement serves and the most commonly used methods</li>
<li>Knowledge of limitations and new problems created when using/overusing these techniques</li>
</ul>

<h2>Segmentation</h2>
<ul>
<li>Awareness of different types of segmentation approaches and strengths of each</li>
<li>Understanding of when to use automatic methods and when they might fail</li>
</ul>

<h2>Shape Analysis</h2>
<li>Knowledge of which types of metrics are easily calculated for shapes in 2D and 3D</li>
<li>Ability to describe a physical measurement problem in terms of shape metrics</li>
<li>Awareness of standard metrics and how they are computed for arbitrary shapes</li>

<h2>Statistics / Big Data</h2>
<li>Awareness of statistical techniques for hypothesis testing</li>
<li>Ability to design basic experiments to test a hypothesis</li>
<li>Ability to analyze and critique poorly designed imaging experiments</li>
<li>Familiarity with vocabulary, tools, and main concepts of big data</li>
<li>Awareness of the differences between normal and big data approaches</li>
<li>Ability to explain MapReduce and apply it to a simple problem</li>
